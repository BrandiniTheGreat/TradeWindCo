# Trade Wind & Co. — Complete Workflow

**From "I have a buyer" to "keys handed over and money in the bank."**

Written as a single narrative for deal #1, when you're building the business and executing the deal simultaneously. Real example throughout: buyer interested in **Araume IV** (2019 Leopard 40, BVI ask $309K, your delivered list $475K).

**Total elapsed time: ~10-12 weeks from buyer interest to keys in their hand.**

Every step explained in plain English. Money flow called out at each phase. Common screw-ups noted.

---

## DAY 0 — You have a buyer

It's a Tuesday morning. Someone fills out the form on your website. Or you get a phone call. Or a sailing club connection texts you: *"Hey, my friend Mark is looking at a Caribbean catamaran. Can he talk to you?"*

That's the moment. Everything from here is execution.

**What you do immediately:**
1. Call him back same day (within 4 hours ideally). Voicemail-to-callback that's longer than a few hours kills 30% of leads.
2. Ask 6 questions:
   - "What's your budget range?" (filters tire-kickers)
   - "What's your timeline — when would you want to be on the boat?" (gauges urgency)
   - "Have you owned a boat before?" (tells you what they need walked through)
   - "Are you cash or financing?" (filters: financing buyers add 2-4 weeks)
   - "Where would you want it delivered?" (FL? NC? somewhere you can deliver?)
   - "How did you find us?" (validates your marketing channels)
3. Walk him through the boat he asked about. The full pitch: ex-Moorings, 3,120 engine hours, four cabins, in Tortola right now, fully documented logbook. $475K duty-paid to his marina.
4. Send him the boat page link + your full landed cost breakdown. He needs to see the math.

**What the buyer is thinking:** *"This guy actually picked up the phone. The numbers are transparent. He knows the boat."*

That's the bar. You don't have to be impressive. You have to be responsive, honest, and prepared.

**Time investment so far: 1 hour.**

## DAY 1-3 — "Is this real?" sound-out + Christian + brother

Now you have one warm lead. Maybe two. You don't know yet if it's a closer.

You're about to make decisions that cost money. Before you spend a dollar, sound out the three people who unblock everything else.

### Day 1 — Call Christian (your attorney friend)

You: *"Hey, remember that catamaran arbitrage thing I told you about? I have a real buyer asking real questions. I want to make this real. Are you in for the legal side?"*

What you need to lock down in this 30-minute call:
- He's on board with 1-2% equity + $1.5K per deal + $250/hr-cap engagement
- He can draft a basic FL LLC operating agreement in 48 hours
- He can review the Trade Wind buyer purchase agreement template
- He's available on call when weird questions come up mid-deal
- (Optional) He's open to applying for the FL Yacht Salesperson License later

**What you walk away with:** verbal commitment. Christian is your legal cover.

### Day 1 — Text your high school friend

You: *"Hey weird question — is your brother still doing yacht sales? Got his number?"*

Friend: *"Yeah, he's still at [marina/brokerage]. Here's his cell."*

That's it. Five minutes of work. You've just opened a door.

### Day 2-3 — Coffee with the brother

You buy coffee. Frame: *"Picking your brain. Exploring something specific."*

What you're really doing: assessing if he's a partner candidate, advisor candidate, or just a referral source. Plus gathering industry intelligence either way.

You ask:
- Is he still actively licensed as a FL Yacht & Ship Broker?
- Has he handled any Caribbean-sourced or imported vessels?
- What's the biggest deal-killer he's seen on imports?
- Would he consider broker-of-record arrangement at $15-20K/year + small equity?

One of three things happens:
1. **He lights up and wants in** → soft commit, paper the broker-of-record agreement within a week. Your regulatory question is solved.
2. **He's interested in advising but not the broker-of-record role** → $500-1,000/month advisor relationship. Get his vendor contacts.
3. **He's not interested** → still walk away with vendor contacts and a market read. Default to owner-exemption strategy for first 2-3 deals.

**What you walk away with:** clarity on whether you have a broker-of-record solution, plus industry intelligence from someone who's done this. Time investment: 60 minutes of coffee.

## DAY 4-7 — Paid expert consults (the small money that prevents big mistakes)

Now you spend ~$500-1,000 to lock down the three questions that determine whether deal #1 nets you $70K or $40K.

### Day 5 — One-hour call with a FL maritime attorney

Cost: $300-500.

This is not Christian. This is someone who does maritime law full-time. Get the referral from Christian or from the brother.

You ask three specific questions and get written answers:

1. **Owner exemption.** "We're going to take title to a foreign-built catamaran in BVI, import it duty-paid to Florida, and immediately resell it. Does FS 326's owner exemption shield us from yacht broker licensing? For how many serial transactions before it stops applying?"

2. **FL sales tax.** "When we deliver the boat to the buyer at a FL marina, does Florida sales tax apply? What if the buyer is a FL resident vs. out-of-state? What's the exemption documentation path?"

3. **CBP transaction value.** "If the buyer wires full delivered price to escrow and our LLC is the importer-of-record buying at the BVI price, does CBP have a basis to argue the buyer's wire amount is the 'true' transaction value, triggering 20% duty on $475K instead of $309K?"

You walk out with a written opinion letter ($300-500). This goes in your file. If anyone ever asks why you operated without a license, or why your duty calculation was $61.8K instead of $95K, this letter is your defense.

### Day 7 — One-hour call with a maritime CPA

Cost: $200-400.

You ask:
- "Given the sales tax framework the attorney just outlined, how should we structure the LLC for tax efficiency?"
- "When should we elect S-corp status?"
- "What's the quarterly estimated tax cadence?"
- "Are startup expenses (attorney fees, licensing) deductible against deal #1's income?"

You walk out with a tax model and a sense of whether to elect S-corp now or wait for revenue. ~$200-400 well spent.

**Time investment Days 4-7: 2 hours on phone, ~$500-900 total cost.**

## DAY 5-10 — Form the business (the boring 2 hours that make this real)

You can do this in parallel with everything else. It takes about 2 hours of total focused time across a week.

### Action 1: File the LLC online

Go to **sunbiz.org** (Florida Division of Corporations).

Click "Start a Business." Fill in:
- LLC name: "Trade Wind & Co., LLC" (verify availability first via sunbiz.org name search)
- Address: your home address or a virtual office
- Registered agent: you (or pay a service $50-150/year for privacy)
- Manager-managed
- Filing fee: $125

Click submit. Pay $125 with credit card. **You now have an LLC.** Document number issued same day.

### Action 2: Get an EIN from the IRS

Go to **irs.gov/EIN**.

Click "Apply Online Now."

Walk through the form:
- "Why are you applying?" → New business
- "Type of business?" → LLC
- "Federal tax classification?" → Single-member or Partnership (depending on whether Chris is on the entity)
- "Why does your business need an EIN?" → Started new business

Click submit. **You have an EIN immediately.** Print the confirmation letter.

### Action 3: Open a business bank account

Walk into Chase Business (or your preferred bank) with:
- LLC articles (from sunbiz)
- EIN letter
- Personal ID
- $100-500 opening deposit

Spend 45 minutes with a banker. Ask:
- "I'm going to be wiring $300-500K amounts both inbound and outbound. What are your daily limits?"
- "Any international wire fees we should know about?"

They open the account. Same day or next business day, you have wire capability. **Trade Wind & Co. is now a real entity with a real bank account.**

**Time investment for Phase B: ~2 hours total.**

## DAY 10-14 — BVI vendor stack (three calls, foreign accents, easier than you think)

Now you start the calls that actually set up the deal mechanics. These are people you'll work with on every deal forever.

### Day 10 — Call the BVI source broker (the specific listing rep)

Example: Moorings Brokerage for Araume IV. You find the rep's number on the listing.

You: *"Hi, I'm calling about Araume IV. I have a buyer in Florida. I want to know if she's still available and what your terms look like."*

You're doing two things: confirming availability and feeling out the seller's flexibility.

Ask these questions:
1. "Is Araume IV still available?"
2. "Is the $309K ask firm, or is there flex on a cash offer with a fast close?"
3. "Title status — is there an active marine mortgage to satisfy?"
4. "What earnest money do you typically expect — 5%, 7%, 10%?"
5. "Can my surveyor get aboard within 2 weeks?"
6. "Who's the closing attorney you typically work with?"
7. "Is there anyone else with a hard offer on her right now?"

The rep is probably friendly and helpful. This is their job. They want to move the boat.

**You walk away with:**
- Boat is available
- Maybe 5-8% flex on the ask
- Title situation clarified
- Surveyor access confirmed
- Closing logistics understood

### Day 12-14 — Call BVI maritime attorney

Recommended: **Conyers Dill & Pearman** (Tortola). Get the referral from your FL maritime attorney.

You: *"Hello, I have a US LLC purchasing a catamaran in the BVI. I need someone to handle the title work, lien search, and bill of sale on our side. Can we set up an engagement?"*

Ask:
1. "What's your fee structure — flat per-deal or hourly?"
2. "What's a realistic close timeline once we have a signed purchase agreement?"
3. "Do you handle the BVI Customs export clearance, or do we use a separate broker?"
4. "What documentation do you need from us to get started?"

Their fees: probably $1,500-2,500 per deal. Wire a small retainer ($1,000-1,500) and you're engaged.

### Day 14 — Call BVI marine surveyor

Recommended: **Sea-Tech Marine** in Road Town.

You: *"I have a buyer interested in Araume IV. I need a sea trial surveyor in two weeks. What's your availability?"*

Ask:
1. "Available dates in the next 2-3 weeks?"
2. "Fee for a 40-foot catamaran sea trial?"
3. "Turnaround on the written report?"
4. "Any relationship with Moorings Brokerage that would be a conflict?"
5. "Direct-bill the buyer, or do we pay you?"

Their fee: probably $1,500-2,500. **Critical: the surveyor bills the buyer directly, not you.** This protects the surveyor's independence and keeps the cost off your books.

**Time investment Days 10-14: 3 hours.**

Now the US-side pieces that actually move the boat from BVI to your buyer's marina.

Call: **Yachtworld Escrow** (or All Points Title).

- Operating agreement

Within 3-5 days, account is open. You now have a third-party escrow agent ready to receive buyer funds.

### Day 14 — US customs broker

Call: **Bayport Customs Brokers** (or whoever your FL maritime attorney recommended).

They quote: probably $800-1,500. Bind 24-48h before departure.

### Day 18 — USCG documentation service

## DAY 38-47 — Ocean passage (you're a project manager, not a sailor)

Captain handles 100% of the ocean leg. You handle communication.

### Daily/weekly cadence

Every 2-3 days, captain sends a satellite update:
- Position
- Sea state
- ETA

You forward sanitized updates to Mark:
*"Mark, update: captain reports position 22°N 70°W, fair winds, on schedule for Lauderdale arrival around [date]."*

This builds trust. Mark feels like the boat is on its way home.

### What can go wrong

- **Weather hold.** Captain shelters at Marsh Harbour or Nassau. Adds 2-5 days. Transit insurance covers it. Update Mark calmly.
- **Mechanical issue.** Captain calls you from satellite phone. You authorize the repair. Comes out of your operating capital or transit insurance depending on cause.
- **Hurricane named.** Captain shelters in a hurricane hole. Adds 1-2 weeks. Same insurance coverage.

These are the moments you earn your margin. Stay calm, communicate clearly, trust the captain.

**Time investment Days 38-47: 3 hours total (mostly weekly buyer updates).**

## DAY 47-52 — US Customs entry (the big duty check)

Captain reports approach to Fort Lauderdale. Now CBP gets involved.

### Day 47 — Captain reports arrival via CBP ROAM app

He files CBP Form 1300 (vessel entrance) remotely. CBP confirms receipt.

### Day 47-48 — Customs broker filings

Email your customs broker: *"Boat arrived Lauderdale. Authorized to file entry."*

Broker files:
- CBP-3461 (entry/immediate delivery) — gets the boat into the country
- CBP-7501 (entry summary) — declares the value and triggers duty calculation

### Day 48-49 — Duty wire

Customs broker calculates: $300,000 acquisition value × 20% = $60,000 duty. Plus harbor maintenance fee ($378) + merchandise processing fee ($1,038). **Total: $61,416.**

You instruct escrow: *"Wire $61,416 to CBP via ACH per attached customs broker instructions."*

Escrow wires. CBP receives. Boat is officially duty-paid.

### Day 49-52 — CBP releases vessel

Within 1-3 days, CBP issues release. Customs broker forwards you the CBP entry packet.

**Mark's boat is now legally a US-import.** You and Mark celebrate quietly via email.

**Time investment Days 47-52: 2 hours.**

## Common screw-ups to avoid

1. **Don't promise a delivery date.** Promise a 10-14 week window. Weather and CBP own the schedule, not you.
2. **Don't take a deposit without an escrow.** Money to your personal account = personal liability + tax mess + buyer skittishness.
3. **Don't skip the title search to save $200.** It will cost you $20K when something surfaces post-close.
4. **Don't route delivery to a non-Lauderdale port to "save time."** Lauderdale CBP is faster. Always.
5. **Don't pay vendors out of pocket.** Every per-deal cost should come from buyer's escrow. Your operating capital stays for emergencies only.
6. **Don't disclose the source broker's name to your buyer publicly.** Watermark photos, use internal listing IDs. Otherwise the buyer Googles, finds the BVI listing, and tries to bypass you.
7. **Don't promise specialist expertise you don't have.** Your value is coordination, not yacht engineering. If a buyer asks technical questions, defer to the surveyor's report.
8. **Don't skip the weekly Mark update.** Anxious buyers walk. Calm communication preserves the deal.
