## 1B. Licensing — FL Yacht & Ship Broker Act (FS 326) (Week 3-12)

**This is the longest-lead item. Start now.** Florida requires anyone who "sells, offers to sell, negotiates the sale of, or leases yachts for compensation" to be licensed. Yacht = 32+ feet. All our inventory qualifies. Operating without a license is a third-degree felony with criminal penalties.

| Task | Owner | Cost | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Determine broker vs. dealer status with maritime attorney | Attorney | (included in retainer) | 1 week |
| Apply for FL Yacht & Ship Broker License (DBPR) | Founder | $700-1,500 application + exam fee | 30-90 days for issuance |
| Pass FL yacht broker exam | Founder | $50-150 exam fee | 2-4 weeks study |
| **Surety bond $25,000 (broker)** | Bond carrier | $250-500/year premium | 5-7 days |
| Open dedicated trust/escrow account at FL bank | Founder | $0-50 setup | 1 week |
| Register trust account with FL DBPR | Founder | $0 | 1-2 weeks |
| Display license & bond info on website + premises | Founder | $0 | — |

**Critical nuance — broker vs. dealer:**
- **Broker** = facilitates sale between buyer and seller for compensation (typical real-estate-style transaction)
- **Dealer** = takes title to vessels and resells on own account (our likely structure if we follow the BVI-close model)

The audit was right that this needs a maritime attorney call. If we're a **dealer** (take title in BVI, resell to US buyer), FS 326 still applies but the framing differs. Some operational details — sales tax handling, trust account requirements, disclosures — differ between the two. Get the determination on paper before applying.

**Continuing education:** FL requires periodic CE for license renewal. Budget 8-16 hours/year and ~$200-400 in course fees.

# PART 2 — Per-Deal Execution (recurring, ~8 weeks per deal)

Assumes Part 1 is fully complete. Buyer-funded escrow model (Option A): buyer wires full delivered price to Yachtworld Escrow at signing; escrow disburses to vendors at each milestone; Trade Wind margin releases on handover.

## Phase 0 — Inquiry to Inspection (Day 0-7)

**What the broker does:**
- **Day 0:** Inquiry lands (phone, web form, email). Same-day callback. Qualify: budget, timeline, port of delivery, USCG citizenship, boating background, financing status.
- **Day 1:** Email pre-inspection packet — survey scope, fee structure, inspection-window options for the specific boat, sample purchase agreement (DocuSign link).
- **Day 2-3:** Confirm inspection mode:
  - **In-person sea trial:** book Sea-Tech Marine (Road Town). Coordinate flights, hotel rec (Wyndham Tortola or Scrub Island), slip access via source broker.
  - **Remote video walkthrough:** schedule 90-min live call with surveyor. Test their video setup the day before.
- **Day 4-7:** Inspection executes. Surveyor sends written report within 48h to buyer (cc broker).

**Parties:** Buyer · BVI surveyor · source broker rep · you
**Cash flow:** Survey fee ($1,500-2,500) billed direct to buyer at booking. We don't carry it.
**Documents:** Pre-inspection packet · NDA if requested · survey scope sheet
**Risk checkpoint:** Survey report. Material defects = buyer walks, no harm. Minor = negotiate.

## Phase 1 — Buyer Commitment to BVI Lockup (Day 7-14)

**What the broker does:**
- **Day 7-9:** Buyer reviews survey. Call to walk through findings.
- **Day 9-10:** Buyer signs Trade Wind purchase agreement at delivered list price. DocuSign.
- **Day 10:** Send wire instructions for Yachtworld Escrow. Wire goes direct to escrow, **not to us**.
- **Day 10-12:** Buyer wires full delivered price (or first stage if 40/40/20 staged) to escrow.
- **Day 12:** Funds clear. Escrow confirms in writing. **Green light** to trigger the BVI offer.
- **Day 12-14:** US maritime attorney drafts BVI purchase agreement. Earnest money (5-10% of BVI ask) wires from our escrow to BVI escrow. Boat is contractually locked.

**Parties:** Buyer · US escrow · US maritime attorney · source broker · BVI maritime attorney · BVI escrow
**Cash flow:** Buyer → US escrow (full or staged). US escrow → BVI escrow (earnest money only, $15-30K).
**Documents:** Trade Wind purchase agreement · BVI purchase agreement · proof-of-funds letter
**Risk checkpoint:** Pull BVI Ships Registry title search BEFORE wiring earnest. $200, 1 day. If active marine mortgage, request payoff statement. Don't proceed if unreleased non-mortgage liens.

## Phase 2 — BVI Close + Export (Day 14-25)

**What the broker does:**
- **Day 14-18:** Title clean → proceed. Mortgage exists → confirm payoff timing with lender, get wire instructions.
- **Day 18-21:** BVI close. Bill of sale executes. Funds flow:
  - Our escrow → BVI escrow: balance of acquisition
  - BVI escrow → marine mortgage lender (if any): payoff
  - BVI escrow → seller: net proceeds
  - Lien release files with BVI Ships Registry
  - Title transfers
- **Day 21-25:** BVI Customs export clearance, Road Town. Declaration of departure, crew list, voyage plan.

**Parties:** Source broker · BVI seller · BVI maritime attorney · BVI Ships Registry · mortgage lender (if any) · BVI Customs
**Cash flow:** Our escrow → BVI escrow → seller (and lender if mortgage)
**Documents:** Bill of sale · lien release · BVI export clearance · voyage plan
**Risk checkpoint:** Mortgage payoff statements valid 10-15 days. If close slides, get fresh statement.

## Phase 3 — Ocean Delivery Prep + Departure (Day 25-37)

**What the broker does:**
- **Day 25-28:** Dispatch delivery captain. Verify USCG license, TWIC, crew booked, passports on file.
- **Day 26:** Weather routing review (Chris Parker or PassageWeather).
- **Day 27:** Transit insurance binder active.
- **Day 28:** Provisioning, rigging check, fuel/water top-up.
- **Day 28-30:** Weather window opens. Depart Road Town. Standard route: Tortola → PR → Turks & Caicos → Bahamas → Fort Lauderdale (~770nm, 7-9 days).
- **Day 30-37:** Daily check-ins via Iridium GO. Forward sanitized updates to buyer weekly.

**Parties:** Delivery captain + crew · insurance broker · weather routing service
**Cash flow:** Captain 30% on dispatch (~$3K), 70% on arrival (~$8K). Transit insurance ~$800-1,500.
**Documents:** Delivery contract · transit insurance cert · voyage plan
**Risk checkpoint:** Hurricane season Jun 1 - Nov 30. Shelter ports pre-identified (Marsh Harbour, Nassau, Stuart). Insurance covers extended layover.

## Phase 4 — US Customs Entry (Day 37-42)

**What the broker does:**
- **Day 37:** Captain reports via CBP ROAM app. CBP Form 1300 filed.
- **Day 37-38:** Customs broker files CBP-3461 (entry/immediate delivery).
- **Day 38-39:** CBP-7501 entry summary filed. Wire 20% duty via ACH (~$61.8K on $309K acquisition + HMF $389 + MPF $1,070 = ~$63,259).
- **Day 39-42:** CBP releases vessel.

**Parties:** CBP (Fort Lauderdale) · customs broker · captain
**Cash flow:** Escrow → CBP (~$63K). Customs broker fee ($500-1,500) separate.
**Documents:** CBP Form 1300 · CBP-3461 · CBP-7501 · bill of sale · commercial invoice
**Risk checkpoint:** Route to Fort Lauderdale only. Lauderdale CBP clears in 1-3 days; smaller ports 5-10 days.

## Phase 5 — USCG Documentation + Final Delivery (Day 42-50, USCG Day ~125)

**What the broker does:**
- **Day 42:** USCG CG-1258 filed with NVDC (Falling Waters, WV). Fees via Pay.gov.
- **Day 42-45:** Yard turnover at Lauderdale. Wash, system check, minor cosmetic.
- **Day 45:** Final buyer wire clears (if staged). Disbursements execute. **Trade Wind margin wires to operating account.**
- **Day 45-48:** Ferry to buyer's home marina.
- **Day 48-50:** Walkthrough at buyer's slip. Hand over keys, surveyor report, CBP entry packet, temporary USCG documentation, transit insurance cert, manuals, equipment inventory. Buyer signs delivery acknowledgment.
- **Day ~125:** Permanent USCG Certificate of Documentation mails to buyer.

**Parties:** USCG NVDC · yard service · captain · buyer
**Cash flow:** Final disbursements + Trade Wind margin
**Documents:** CG-1258 · delivery acknowledgment · equipment inventory
**Risk checkpoint:** NVDC backlog 60-90 days. Temp docs cover the gap legally. Follow up monthly.

## Common stalls + your playbook

1. **Buyer won't wire 100% upfront** → Fall back 40/40/20: signing / customs / handover.
2. **BVI seller demands higher earnest** → 10% maximum, never more.
3. **Survey finds engine compression issue** → Renegotiate or walk. Below 80% spec = $30-40K repower risk.
4. **Marine mortgage on title** → Standard close mechanic. Confirm payoff timing 5 days before close.
5. **Hurricane in delivery window** → Captain shelters in Bahamas. Insurance extends. Built into delivery line item.
6. **CBP delay** → Customs broker's job. Worst case 5-10 days at smaller port. We absorb.
7. **NVDC 4-month backlog** → Buyer operates on temp docs (legal). Apologize, no discount — federal issue.
8. **Buyer asks to inspect in FL before final wire** → Yes. Build 1-day FL inspection window before final wire. Contract allows credit or walk.
9. **Source broker drops price after our buyer signs** → Our contract locked the delivered price. We pocket extra margin. Don't pass through (we already gave the buyer the savings vs comp).
10. **Sales tax surprise** → Pre-cleared with maritime CPA. Don't structure deals without that determination first.

## What to NOT do

- **Never** acquire a boat speculatively. Always pre-sold with deposit in escrow.
- **Never** wire personal capital to BVI seller. Use escrow always.
- **Never** skip the BVI Ships Registry title search to save $200. It will cost you $20K when something surfaces post-close.
- **Never** route a delivery to a non-Lauderdale port "to save time." It won't.
- **Never** disclose source broker pricing to your buyer. They'll Google. They'll find. They'll try to bypass. (Watermark photos. Keep BVI listing IDs off the public boat page.)
- **Never** quote a price that excludes 20% duty. Always quote delivered.
- **Never** operate without the FL license. Felony.
