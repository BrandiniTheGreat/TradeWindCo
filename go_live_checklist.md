# Trade Wind & Co. — Go-Live Call List

**Trigger:** site is up, you've got 1-2 buyers with real interest (asked about a specific boat, asked about timeline, asked about pricing). This is the order of operations to convert that interest into a closed deal while building the regulated business around it.

**Total elapsed: ~3 weeks of calls and paperwork to get from "warm lead" to "buyer wired to escrow." Most calls are 15-30 minutes. Total founder time: 25-40 hours spread over 3 weeks.**

---

## Pre-flight — have this ready before you start dialing

- [ ] Specific boat ID the buyer is interested in (full source broker info, BVI ask, US retail comp)
- [ ] Buyer's contact info, financing readiness (pre-approved or cash?), target destination port
- [ ] Approximate timeline buyer wants (urgency level — "next month" vs "next year")
- [ ] Buyer's boating background (USCG-licensed? chartered before? new owner?)
- [ ] Your laptop with the demo site, broker_playbook.md, and this checklist open

## PHASE A — Lock the regulatory frame (Days 1-7)

These calls happen first because they determine the legal structure everything else hangs on.

### A1. Christian — your attorney friend (Day 1, 30-45 min)

**Why:** confirm his role, scope of his involvement, compensation arrangement. He's the only call that doesn't need anyone else to be in place first.

**Questions to get answered:**
1. Are you in on the 1-2% equity + $1.5K/deal + $250/hr cap arrangement?
2. Can you draft the FL LLC operating agreement (single or multi-member depending on Chris)?
3. Can you draft / review the Trade Wind buyer purchase agreement template?
4. Are you willing to apply for FL Yacht & Ship Salesperson license? (Optional — separate ask)
5. What's your conflict-of-interest check with your current firm — does this trigger anything?
6. Can you be on call for "weird question" moments mid-deal?
7. Realistic turnaround on contract drafting — 48 hours or longer?

**Expected outcome:** signed engagement letter or handshake agreement, scope defined, his timeline confirmed.

### A2. High school friend — to intro the brother (Day 1, 5 min)

**Why:** soft intro before reaching out to the brother directly. Friendlier than cold-calling.

**Questions to ask:**
1. Is your brother still in yacht sales?
2. What's he up to these days — still in FL?
3. Got a phone number / preferred contact?
4. Any heads-up on his current situation (job satisfaction, looking for opportunities, retired, etc.)?
5. Mind if I reach out about a project?

**Expected outcome:** warm intro and contact info. 24-hour callback from brother expected.

### A3. Brother — coffee meeting (Day 3-7, 60 min)

**Why:** assess fit, gauge interest in broker-of-record role, gather industry intel either way.

**Frame:** *"Picking your brain on the industry. I'm exploring something specific and could use 30-60 minutes of your time. Coffee's on me."*

**Questions to get answered:**
1. Are you currently active in yacht sales? If so, where, and what's your role?
2. Do you hold a current FL Yacht & Ship Broker License? Salesperson?
3. (If currently employed) Does your employment agreement allow side broker-of-record arrangements?
4. Have you handled Caribbean-sourced or imported vessels before?
5. Who's your customs broker network in Florida?
6. Who's a great Caribbean → FL delivery captain you've worked with?
7. What's the most common deal-killer you've seen on imports?
8. What's your read on the 2025 tariff arbitrage — is anyone actively pumping BVI inventory yet?
9. If you were going to do this with someone, what would the deal need to look like for you?
10. Would you consider broker-of-record at $15-20K/year + small equity, or is that too much/too little hassle?

**Expected outcome:** one of three:
- He's in → soft commit on broker-of-record arrangement, paper it within 7 days
- He's interested in advising → $500-1K/month advisor relationship, no broker license use
- He's not interested → get his vendor contacts and parting blessing; explore alternatives

### A4. FL maritime attorney — paid 1-hour consult (Day 5-10, $300-500)

**Why:** the three risk questions Christian probably can't answer with certainty. Worth paying for written opinions on file.

**Questions to get answered (in this order — high to low priority):**
1. **Owner exemption defensibility.** "If our LLC takes title to a foreign-built catamaran in BVI, imports it duty-paid to FL, and immediately resells it to a US buyer, does FS 326's owner exemption (§ 326.004(2)) shield us from yacht broker licensing requirements? For how many serial transactions? Where does the line break?"
2. **FL sales tax model.** "BVI acquisition → FL port of entry → buyer takes delivery in FL marina → buyer is (a) FL resident or (b) non-FL resident. What's our FL sales tax exposure in each case? Documentation path for exemption?"
3. **CBP transaction value risk.** "Customer wires full price to escrow, escrow pays BVI seller acquisition price, we pay 20% duty on acquisition price. If CBP audits and argues customer's wire is the 'true' transaction value, what's our defense? Does the LLC need to hold title for any minimum period?"
4. **BVI maritime attorney recommendation.** "Who do you work with for cross-border yacht transactions? Conyers Dill & Pearman? Other?"
5. **Customs broker recommendation.** "Who do you trust for vessel-class CBP entries at Fort Lauderdale?"

**Expected outcome:** written opinion letter on owner exemption + sales tax framework + customs broker referral. ~$300-500 well spent.

### A5. Maritime CPA — paid 1-hour consult (Day 7-10, $200-400)

**Why:** sales tax model + tax structuring before deal #1 closes.

**Questions to get answered:**
1. FL sales tax treatment of our transaction structure (cross-reference attorney's opinion)
2. S-corp election timing — wait until revenue or elect immediately?
3. Quarterly estimated tax cadence for Year 1
4. Deductibility of startup expenses (attorney fees, licensing, marketing)
5. Recommended bookkeeping setup (QuickBooks Online + which chart of accounts)
6. Any state tax surprises beyond sales tax we should plan for?

**Expected outcome:** tax framework + S-corp decision + bookkeeping setup recommendation.

## PHASE B — Form the entity (Days 5-10, runs in parallel with Phase A)

### B1. FL Division of Corporations — file LLC online (Day 5, 30 min, $125)

**Where:** sunbiz.org

**What to file:**
- Articles of Organization
- Trade Wind & Co., LLC (verify name availability first)
- Registered agent: you initially, or pay a service ($50-150/year)
- Manager-managed structure (you + Chris as managers if partnered)

**Expected outcome:** LLC formed same day, document number issued.

### B2. IRS — apply for EIN online (Day 5, 15 min, free)

**Where:** irs.gov, EIN online application

**Expected outcome:** EIN issued instantly, can immediately open bank account.

### B3. Business bank account — local FL bank or Chase (Day 7-10, 1 hour)

**Recommended:** Chase Business (national footprint, marine industry familiar) or a FL community bank.

**Bring to the appointment:**
- LLC articles
- EIN letter
- Personal ID
- Initial deposit ($100-500)
- Operating agreement (Christian's draft)

**Questions to ask:**
1. Wire transfer limits (need to support $300-500K wires for deal economics)
2. International wire fees and processing times
3. Trust account capability if we hold escrow (probably not, but ask)
4. Mobile deposit + wire approval workflow
5. Any marine-industry merchant services they offer?

**Expected outcome:** business checking account open, wire capability confirmed.

## PHASE C — BVI vendor stack (Days 10-21)

### C1. BVI source broker — for the specific boat (Day 10, 30 min)

**Examples:** Moorings Brokerage, BVI Yacht Sales, The Multihull Company, Just Catamarans

**Who to call:** the specific listing's contact rep.

**Questions to get answered:**
1. Is the boat still available?
2. Confirm current BVI ask price
3. Title status — clean? Any active marine mortgage to satisfy?
4. Originating fleet (Moorings YOP? Sunsail? Dream Yacht?) and phase-out date
5. Survey allotment — what's required to get a surveyor aboard, what's the lead time?
6. Earnest money percentage they expect (5-10%?)
7. Recommended close timeline if we move fast (7 days? 14? 21?)
8. Cash offer flex — would seller entertain offers below ask?
9. Recommended closing attorney they typically work with
10. Are there other interested buyers in the pipeline? (Manufactures urgency cue)

**Expected outcome:** confirmed availability, price flex understood, surveyor logistics clear.

### C2. BVI maritime attorney (Day 12-14, 30 min)

**Recommended:** Conyers Dill & Pearman (Tortola) — institutional, fast, expensive but reliable. Get referral from your FL maritime attorney.

**Questions to get answered:**
1. Engagement letter + fee structure (per-deal flat vs. hourly)
2. Bill of sale + transfer template they use for non-resident purchasers
3. BVI Ships Registry title search procedure + fee
4. Lien release filing process if marine mortgage exists
5. BVI Customs export clearance handling (do they do it or refer out?)
6. Estimated total close-side cost per deal (target: under $2,500)
7. Turnaround on title search (1-2 days?)
8. Wire/escrow account they use for BVI close

**Expected outcome:** retainer signed, fee structure known, contact established for all BVI close work.

### C3. BVI marine surveyor (Day 14, 20 min)

**Recommended:** Sea-Tech Marine, Road Town (Tortola)

**Questions to get answered:**
1. Availability windows for the buyer's preferred dates
2. Fee structure (typical $1,500-2,500 for a 40-45ft catamaran)
3. Report deliverable timeline (target: 48h from sea trial)
4. In-person sea trial vs. remote video walkthrough options
5. Current backlog — how far out are bookings?
6. Conflict of interest check (any relationship with the source broker?)
7. Engagement letter / direct billing to buyer
8. Coverage scope — hull, engines, rigging, electrical, electronics?

**Expected outcome:** survey scheduled or held; engagement letter signed; buyer billing direct.

## PHASE D — US vendor stack (Days 14-21)

### D1. US maritime escrow — Yachtworld Escrow or All Points Title (Day 14, 30 min)

**Recommended:** Yachtworld Escrow (institutional, BVI brokers recognize them).

**Questions to get answered:**
1. Master account opening process for an LLC dealer
2. Milestone-based disbursement structure (BVI seller, CBP, captain, customs, our margin)
3. Wire fees per disbursement
4. Hold periods or restrictions on disbursement
5. Wire fraud insurance — what's covered and what's not
6. Required documentation per disbursement (bill of sale, CBP-7501, captain receipt, etc.)
7. Turnaround on disbursement requests (24h? same-day?)
8. International wire handling for BVI close

**Expected outcome:** master account open, disbursement workflow documented.

### D2. US customs broker (Day 14, 30 min)

**Recommended:** Bayport Customs Brokers (Fort Lauderdale) — get the actual referral from your FL maritime attorney.

**Questions to get answered:**
1. Account opening process for a vessel-importing LLC
2. Vessel-class entry experience (how many vessel imports/year do they handle?)
3. Fee structure per entry ($500-1,500 typical)
4. Turnaround time from arrival to CBP release (target: 1-3 days at Lauderdale)
5. Pre-arrival document review service (do they review BVI bill of sale + commercial invoice before vessel arrives?)
6. ACH wire setup for duty payment to CBP
7. Their experience with the 20% tariff on foreign-built vessels (it's new — make sure they're current)
8. Backup port recommendation if Lauderdale is backed up

**Expected outcome:** account opened, fee structure known, ready to engage on deal.

### D3. Delivery captain — Caribbean → FL (Day 14-21, 30 min)

**Recommended:** Eddie M. or alternates from Caribbean Yacht Brokers Network roster. Get referral from brother if possible.

**Questions to get answered:**
1. USCG license type + currency (Master 100T minimum)
2. TWIC card current?
3. Caribbean → FL experience — how many passages, what routes?
4. Standard crew size (typically 2-3 + captain)
5. Crew passport status, BVI work eligibility
6. Standard route preference (Tortola → PR → Turks & Caicos → Bahamas → FL)
7. Fee structure: total quote, dispatch/arrival split (typical 30/70)
8. Insurance proof (P&I, liability)
9. Communications: Iridium GO, daily check-in cadence?
10. Hurricane window contingency plan
11. Availability for upcoming windows

**Expected outcome:** standing contractor agreement, fee structure agreed, ready to dispatch on deal.

### D4. Transit insurance broker (Day 16, 20 min)

**Recommended:** Pantaenius or Markel — marine specialists.

**Questions to get answered:**
1. Marine hull policy for delivery transit — quote process
2. Coverage limits (typical hull value coverage: agreed value)
3. Named storm exclusions — what dates? What zones?
4. Wreck removal coverage included?
5. Premium estimate for typical BVI → FL passage on a $400K hull
6. Binder turnaround (target: 48h)
7. Quote process for ongoing relationship

**Expected outcome:** quote ready, binder process clear.

### D5. USCG documentation service (Day 18, 15 min)

**Recommended:** Maritime Documentation Center, or self-file via NVDC.

**Questions to get answered:**
1. Account setup
2. CG-1258 filing process for a foreign-built vessel
3. Current NVDC processing backlog (probably 60-90 days)
4. Citizenship verification for LLC ownership
5. Fee per filing ($400-800)
6. Temporary documentation handling during NVDC wait
7. Foreign-built vessel quirks (any special endorsements needed?)

**Expected outcome:** account open, filing template understood.

## PHASE E — Engage the buyer formally (Days 14-21)

By this point, all the vendor pieces are in place. Now you're closing the buyer.

### E1. Send buyer pre-inspection packet (Day 14, 30 min)

**Email contents:**
- Boat detail page link (your site)
- Survey scope sheet
- Surveyor fee structure (billed direct from surveyor to buyer)
- Inspection mode options (in-person sea trial or remote video walkthrough)
- Sample Trade Wind purchase agreement (Christian's draft)
- Sample escrow disbursement timeline
- Inspection windows available
- Pre-approval letter request (if financing)

**Expected outcome:** buyer reviews; books inspection within 7 days.

### E2. Buyer confirmation call (Day 17-20, 30 min)

**Questions to confirm with buyer:**
1. Inspection mode chosen (in-person or remote)?
2. Preferred date window?
3. Financing status (cash or pre-approved)?
4. Destination port confirmed?
5. Any specific concerns about the boat from the spec sheet?
6. Ready to sign Trade Wind purchase agreement subject to satisfactory inspection?

**Expected outcome:** verbal commitment, ready for DocuSign signature.

### E3. Send purchase agreement via DocuSign (Day 20, 15 min)

**Contents:**
- Buyer name, contact, boat details
- Delivered list price
- Inspection contingency
- Escrow disbursement schedule
- Default and remedy provisions
- Force majeure / hurricane clauses
- Christian-reviewed terms

**Expected outcome:** buyer signs within 3-5 days.

### E4. Wire instructions for Yachtworld Escrow (Day 21, 10 min)

**Email contents:**
- Yachtworld Escrow wire information
- Reference: buyer name + boat ID
- Amount: full delivered price (or 40% if staged)
- Expected receipt confirmation timeline

**Expected outcome:** buyer wires within 5-7 days. Funds clear. **This is the green light.**

## Cadence note

These calls don't all have to happen Day 1. Realistic spacing:

- **Days 1-3:** Christian, high school friend, brother coffee → regulatory frame mostly clear
- **Days 4-7:** FL maritime attorney + CPA consults → tax + licensing certainty
- **Days 5-10:** LLC + bank + business setup → entity in place
- **Days 10-14:** BVI vendors → source broker, attorney, surveyor lined up
- **Days 14-18:** US vendors → escrow, customs broker, captain, insurance, USCG service
- **Days 18-21:** Buyer engagement → packet, contract, escrow wire
- **Day 21+:** Buyer wires, vendor stack executes, 8-week deal clock starts

If you compress this to 2 weeks because the buyer is impatient, fine. If it stretches to 4 weeks because vendors take longer to set up, also fine. The order matters more than the timing.
